One-Pot Broccoli Mac and Cheese

Skip the the roux and the extra pan. Thickened with cornstarch, this cozy one-pot broccoli mac and cheese recipe comes together in just 35 minutes.

One-Pot Broccoli Mac and Cheese

Recipe

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add in your favorite pasta (like elbow macaroni) and cook according to package instructions until it's just al dente. While the pasta is cooking, chop up some fresh broccoli into bite-sized florets. When the pasta is almost done, toss the broccoli florets into the pot with the pasta and let them cook together for about 2-3 minutes until the broccoli is tender but still slightly crisp. Once the pasta and broccoli are cooked, drain them and return them to the pot. Now it's time for the cheesy goodness! Stir in some shredded cheddar cheese, a dollop of butter, a splash of milk, and a pinch of salt and pepper to taste. Keep stirring until the cheese is melted and everything is well combined. You can adjust the amount of cheese and milk based on how creamy you want your mac and cheese to be. Feel free to add in extras like cooked bacon, diced tomatoes, or a sprinkle of breadcrumbs on top for some extra flavor and crunch. Serve your one-pot broccoli mac and cheese hot, and enjoy a comforting and satisfying meal with minimal cleanup!

Skillet Chicken Thighs and Peaches

Frozen peaches make this one-pan chicken dinner a breeze to throw together; you’ll want bread or potatoes on the side to sop up all the luscious sauce.

Skillet Chicken Thighs and Peaches

Recipe

Start by seasoning bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and any of your favorite herbs or spices. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add a bit of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, place the chicken thighs in the skillet, skin side down, and cook for about 5-6 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the chicken thighs over and cook for another 5-6 minutes until they're cooked through and nicely browned on both sides. Remove the chicken thighs from the skillet and set them aside. In the same skillet, add a bit more olive oil if needed, then add sliced peaches and cook for about 2-3 minutes until they start to caramelize and soften slightly. You can also add a touch of honey or brown sugar to enhance the sweetness of the peaches. Once the peaches are caramelized, return the chicken thighs to the skillet, nestling them among the peaches. Pour in a bit of chicken broth or white wine to deglaze the skillet and create a flavorful sauce. Let everything simmer together for a few minutes until the sauce has thickened slightly and the chicken is heated through. Garnish the skillet chicken thighs and peaches with some fresh herbs like thyme or parsley, and serve hot. This dish pairs beautifully with a side of rice, couscous, or crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce. Enjoy the sweet and savory flavors in every bite!

Ginger Fried Rice

This quick, fragrant comfort food is what nourished cookbook author Hetty Lui McKinnon after giving birth to her daughter.

Ginger Fried Rice

Recipe

Start by cooking your rice according to package instructions and set it aside to cool. You can use leftover rice for this recipe, as it works best when it's chilled. In a skillet or wok, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add finely chopped ginger and minced garlic to the hot oil and sauté for about a minute until fragrant. Next, add diced vegetables of your choice, such as carrots, bell peppers, peas, and onions. Stir-fry the vegetables until they are tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and crack eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they're cooked through, then mix them with the vegetables. Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps with a spoon. Pour in some soy sauce and stir-fry everything together until the rice is heated through and evenly coated with the sauce. You can adjust the amount of soy sauce to your taste. For extra flavor, you can also add some diced cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu to the fried rice. Finally, garnish your ginger fried rice with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added texture and flavor. Serve the ginger fried rice hot as a satisfying main dish or as a tasty side dish alongside your favorite Asian-inspired meal. Enjoy the wonderful blend of ginger, garlic, and soy sauce in every bite!

Gluten-Free Focaccia with Rosemary and Onion

Psyllium husk is the game-changing ingredient that transforms this easy focaccia recipe from “good for gluten-free” to “absolutely amazing.”.

Gluten-Free Focaccia with Rosemary and Onion

Recipe

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C) and greasing a baking sheet with olive oil. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of gluten-free all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of xanthan gum (if not already included in your flour blend), 1 tablespoon of baking powder, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix well to combine. In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 cup of warm water, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and 1 teaspoon of honey (or maple syrup). Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until a sticky dough forms. Transfer the dough onto the greased baking sheet and use wet hands to press it out into a rectangle or oval shape, about 1/2 inch thick. Brush the top of the dough with additional olive oil, then sprinkle chopped fresh rosemary and thinly sliced onions evenly over the surface. Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through. You can test for doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center – it should come out clean. Once baked, remove the focaccia from the oven and let it cool slightly on the baking sheet before slicing and serving. Enjoy this gluten-free focaccia with rosemary and onion as a delicious side dish, snack, or accompaniment to your favorite soups and salads. The combination of fragrant rosemary and savory onion makes for a flavorful and satisfying treat!

Ginger-Garlic Chicken and Vegetable Stir Fry

Level up your weeknight dinner game with this fragrant, veggie-packed stir-fry.

Ginger-Garlic Chicken and Vegetable Stir Fry

Recipe

Start by slicing boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs into thin strips. Marinate the chicken strips in a mixture of minced ginger, minced garlic, soy sauce, and a splash of rice vinegar for about 15-30 minutes. While the chicken is marinating, prepare your vegetables. You can use a variety of vegetables such as bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snap peas, and mushrooms. Slice them into bite-sized pieces so they cook quickly and evenly. Heat some oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated chicken strips to the skillet and stir-fry until they are cooked through and lightly browned. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add a bit more oil if needed, then add the sliced vegetables. Stir-fry the vegetables for a few minutes until they are tender-crisp but still vibrant in color. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet with the vegetables. Pour in any remaining marinade from the chicken and stir everything together. For extra flavor and thickness, you can mix together a cornstarch slurry by combining cornstarch with cold water, then add it to the skillet. Stir well until the sauce thickens slightly. Season the stir fry with a pinch of salt and pepper to taste. You can also add a drizzle of sesame oil for additional flavor if desired. Garnish the ginger-garlic chicken and vegetable stir fry with chopped green onions or cilantro before serving. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles for a delicious and satisfying meal packed with vibrant flavors and nutrients!

Herby Cauliflower Fritters

Frozen cauliflower deserves to be more than a side. Here a quick zap in the microwave primes the veggie crumbles to become a hearty vegetarian main.

Herby Cauliflower Fritters

Recipe

Start by steaming or boiling cauliflower florets until they're tender. Drain them well and then mash them with a fork or potato masher until they're mostly smooth but still have some texture. Transfer the mashed cauliflower to a large mixing bowl and add chopped fresh herbs of your choice. You can use parsley, cilantro, dill, chives, or a combination of your favorites. Add in minced garlic, a pinch of salt, pepper, and your preferred spices for extra flavor. Next, crack in a couple of eggs and mix everything together until well combined. Gradually add gluten-free flour or almond flour to the mixture until it forms a thick batter that holds together. Heat some oil in a skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, spoon the cauliflower batter into the skillet to form small fritters. Flatten them slightly with the back of a spoon and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. Once the fritters are cooked through and crispy on the outside, transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil. Serve the herby cauliflower fritters hot, garnished with additional fresh herbs and a squeeze of lemon juice if desired. These fritters make a delicious appetizer, snack, or side dish that's packed with flavor and nutrients!

Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake

Our best gluten-free chocolate cake recipe combines oat and rice flours for a tender fudgy all-occasion dessert. The pudding-like frosting is just a bonus.

Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake

Recipe

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan and line the bottom with parchment paper for easy removal. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together gluten-free all-purpose flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and a pinch of salt until well combined. In another bowl, cream together softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add in eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in some vanilla extract for extra flavor. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with milk, and mixing until a smooth batter forms. Ensure that all ingredients are well incorporated. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles. Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. While the cake is cooling, you can prepare your favorite frosting. You can go for a classic chocolate buttercream or a simple ganache, depending on your preference. Once the cake is completely cooled, frost it generously with your chosen frosting. You can also add some chocolate shavings or sprinkles on top for decoration if you like. Slice and serve this decadent gluten-free chocolate cake to satisfy your sweet cravings. It's moist, rich, and utterly delicious – a perfect treat for any occasion!

Lemony White Bean Soup

Plump white beans create a luscious broth combined with lemon zest, onion, garlic, and celery. A stealthy knob of butter adds just enough richness.

Lemony White Bean Soup

Recipe

Start by heating some olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions, celery, and carrots to the pot, and sauté until they're softened and fragrant, about 5-7 minutes. Next, add minced garlic to the pot and cook for an additional minute until it becomes aromatic. Now, add drained and rinsed canned white beans (such as cannellini or navy beans) to the pot along with vegetable broth. You can also use homemade broth if you prefer. Bring the mixture to a simmer. Once the soup is simmering, add in fresh thyme leaves, a bay leaf, and a pinch of salt and pepper to taste. Let the soup simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. After the soup has simmered, remove the bay leaf from the pot. Then, using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree a portion of the soup until it reaches your desired consistency. This will help thicken the soup while still leaving some whole beans and vegetables for texture. Finally, add freshly squeezed lemon juice to the soup, adjusting the amount to taste depending on how lemony you prefer your soup. Stir well to combine. Serve the lemony white bean soup hot, garnished with a sprinkle of freshly chopped parsley or a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. This soup is light, flavorful, and refreshing, perfect for a comforting meal on a chilly day. Enjoy!
